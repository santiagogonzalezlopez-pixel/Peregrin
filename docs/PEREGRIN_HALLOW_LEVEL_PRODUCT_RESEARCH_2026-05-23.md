# Peregrin - Hallow-level product research

Last updated: 2026-05-23

Purpose: define what would make Peregrin not just functional, but genuinely
excellent: useful, beautiful, specific, shareable, and capable of becoming the
default app for a very focused Catholic pilgrimage niche.

## Executive decision

Peregrin should not try to become "Hallow for audio" or a broad Catholic content
library.

The stronger ambition is:

```text
Peregrin is the Catholic pilgrim passport for real holy places.
```

In practice, that means Peregrin should own the moment when faith becomes place:

- discovering a holy place,
- choosing a pilgrimage route,
- arriving with an intention,
- marking a real visit,
- saving the memory,
- completing a route or country,
- sharing a beautiful Catholic card,
- coming back later to continue the journey.

The Hallow-level idea for Peregrin is not audio. It is a living pilgrimage
experience.

## What Hallow teaches us

Hallow's advantage is not just "Catholic content." It combines:

- a daily habit loop,
- seasonal challenges around Lent, Advent and prayer moments,
- strong audio production and recognizable voices,
- free entry points with a premium upgrade path,
- community and family features,
- institutional growth through parishes, schools and dioceses,
- credible Catholic review of content.

Evidence:

- Hallow reported more than 1 billion prayers completed, nearly 24 million
  installs, presence in 150 countries and eight languages.
- Its Google Play listing emphasizes more than 10,000 sessions, daily prayer,
  Rosary, Lectio Divina, challenges, reminders, offline access, family/community,
  and a paid subscription.

Implication for Peregrin:

We should not copy Hallow's content category. We should copy the strategic shape:

```text
habit + liturgical timing + high polish + shareable milestones + institutions
```

Translated to Peregrin:

```text
routes + feast calendars + pilgrimage memories + passport progress + parish/group outreach
```

## Competitive map

### Broad Catholic prayer/content apps

Hallow, Amen, Ascension and Laudate occupy daily prayer, Bible, readings,
Rosary, catechesis, meditations and audio.

Peregrin should avoid competing directly here. Adding generic prayers, readings
or broad audio would pull us into a crowded lane.

### Camino and pilgrimage logistics apps

Camino Ninja, Buen Camino, Wise Pilgrim and similar apps solve walking logistics:
routes, stages, accommodations, distances, offline data, navigation and practical
tips.

JacoStamp is closer to Peregrin's emotional territory: digital credential,
stamp discovery, and social sharing of stamps and credentials.

### Shrine-specific apps

Shrines such as Fatima have official apps focused on one place: guide, prayer,
schedules, events and accessibility. Rome Pilgrim focuses deeply on Rome with
relics, Mass/confession/adoration schedules, routes and favorites. EWTN Travel
links religious sites with maps, itineraries and videos.

These prove demand for Catholic travel tools, but they are fragmented by place.

### Pilgrimage community apps

Pelegrin is targeting Catholic youth and WYD Seoul 2027 around community,
matching, chat, safety and a web-first experience.

Peregrin should not chase chat or social networking. That is operationally
riskier and less differentiated for us. Peregrin's safer and stronger angle is:

```text
places, passport, routes, certificates, memories, shareable pilgrimage proof
```

## The core product loop Peregrin needs

The app should guide a user through this loop:

```text
Open app
  -> choose a country, shrine, route or feast
  -> open a beautiful pilgrimage guide
  -> set a simple intention
  -> visit/mark a holy place
  -> save one private memory
  -> collect a stamp/card/certificate
  -> share a beautiful image
  -> get invited into the next route or feast
```

This is the equivalent of Hallow's "open, pray, complete, continue" loop.

## The super idea: Pilgrim Mode

Pilgrim Mode is the feature that could make Peregrin feel alive.

For each route, especially flagship routes like Manila Visita Iglesia, Holy Land,
Guadalupe and Rome, the route is not just a list of stops. It becomes a guided
pilgrimage session:

- start screen with route title, map, estimated stops and spiritual theme,
- "My intention for this pilgrimage" private text field,
- stop-by-stop cards with:
  - place image or icon,
  - why this place matters,
  - short prayer,
  - practical note,
  - map/directions button,
  - "I am here" or "I visited" action,
  - private memory note,
- progress ribbon,
- completion card,
- certificate,
- optional Play review request only after completion/share.

First version can be simple and static. It does not need GPS proof or complex
tracking.

Why this is powerful:

- It turns Peregrin from a database into an experience.
- It gives the user a reason to open the app at each stop.
- It creates a satisfying completion moment.
- It creates a shareable artifact.
- It gives us an ethical review moment.

## Seasonal and feast-based growth

Peregrin should have campaigns tied to Catholic time, not random app marketing.

Suggested campaign calendar:

- January: Santo Nino Cebu / Black Nazarene / Philippines devotion.
- Lent and Holy Week: Visita Iglesia, Via Crucis, Seven Churches.
- May: Marian month, Marian shrines.
- July/August: Camino and summer pilgrimage.
- September: Holy Land / Exaltation of the Cross / Our Lady of Sorrows.
- October: Rosary month, Marian routes.
- December: Guadalupe, Advent, Bethlehem/Nativity route.
- 2027 build-up: WYD Seoul routes around Korean martyrs and youth pilgrimage.

Each campaign should have:

- one custom Play Store listing or listing URL,
- one simple landing page,
- one free hero route,
- one beautiful share card,
- one outreach list,
- one measurement dashboard.

## Product pillars

### 1. Living Passport

The passport should become the emotional home of the app:

- countries,
- routes,
- shrine stamps,
- certificates,
- achievements,
- memories,
- timeline of visits,
- "my next pilgrimage" prompt.

It should feel like a digital object users are proud of, not just a stats page.

### 2. Guided Routes

Routes are the most natural product unit:

- Manila Visita Iglesia,
- Santo Nino Cebu,
- Marian Shrines Luzon,
- Holy Land Gospel route,
- Jerusalem Passion route,
- Guadalupe route,
- Rome Seven Churches,
- Papal Basilicas,
- Lourdes and Fatima,
- Camino spiritual route.

Each route should have a strong identity, not just stops.

### 3. Shareable Catholic Beauty

The organic growth loop is sharing:

- route completion cards,
- country completion cards,
- feast pilgrimage cards,
- "I visited..." cards,
- certificate previews for WhatsApp, Instagram Stories and Facebook.

These must look devotional and tasteful. Not meme-like, not touristy, not cheap.

### 4. Packs As Growth Doors

Keep one app, many entry doors:

```text
Peregrin Philippines
Peregrin Holy Land
Peregrin Guadalupe
Peregrin Rome
Peregrin Korea 2027
```

The user installs one app, but the store listing, landing page, screenshots and
campaign promise feel local and specific.

### 5. Privacy-respectful Analytics

Downloads are not enough. We need activation data, without collecting private
journal/prayer text.

Events to track:

- app_opened,
- language_selected,
- country_opened,
- shrine_opened,
- route_opened,
- visit_marked,
- journal_started,
- route_progress_started,
- route_completed,
- certificate_generated,
- share_card_opened,
- share_card_shared,
- premium_modal_opened,
- premium_purchase_started,
- premium_purchase_completed,
- restore_purchase_started,
- restore_purchase_completed.

Firebase Analytics supports up to 500 custom event types, which is more than
enough for this.

### 6. Reviews At The Right Moment

Google Play's in-app review guidance says to request reviews after the user has
experienced enough of the app, and not excessively.

Best Peregrin moments:

- after completing a route,
- after generating a certificate,
- after sharing a card,
- after marking 3 real visits.

Never ask on first launch. Never incentivize reviews.

## Google Play discovery implications in 2026

Google Play is moving toward more AI-assisted discovery:

- app discovery in Gemini,
- Play Shorts,
- Ask Play conversational search,
- AI-generated custom listings from keyword recommendations.

This means Peregrin's store presence should be built around natural user
questions:

- "Catholic pilgrimage passport app"
- "Catholic shrines near me"
- "Visita Iglesia route app"
- "Holy Land Catholic pilgrimage guide"
- "Guadalupe pilgrimage passport"
- "Rome Seven Churches pilgrimage"
- "Catholic travel app for shrines"

Store copy, screenshots and landing pages should answer those queries directly.

## Priority countries and packs

### 1. Philippines

Why:

- Official census data reports 85.6 million Roman Catholics in 2020, 78.8% of
  the household population.
- Android market share in April 2026 was 88.67% in StatCounter.
- Strong living traditions: Visita Iglesia, Santo Nino, Black Nazarene,
  Baclaran, Manaoag, Antipolo, Penafrancia.

What to do:

- Treat Philippines as the first serious acquisition experiment.
- Build Pilgrim Mode first for Manila Visita Iglesia.
- Use a Philippines custom store listing.
- Use English first, then Tagalog/Filipino reviewed by a native Catholic speaker.

### 2. Holy Land

Why:

- Highest universal Catholic resonance.
- The places are spiritually central even for people who may never travel.
- Prestige pack for the brand.

Risks:

- Needs reverent editorial tone.
- Needs sensitivity and no casual/political framing.
- Needs careful place selection.

What to do:

- Make it the prestige pack.
- Do fewer places, better.
- Routes: Gospel of Galilee, Jerusalem Passion, Bethlehem/Nativity.

### 3. Mexico / Guadalupe

Why:

- Spanish advantage.
- Huge Catholic devotion.
- Android market share in Mexico was 69.61% in April 2026.

What to do:

- Build as Spanish-first pack after Philippines instrumentation.
- Center the pack on Guadalupe, not generic Mexico.

### 4. Rome

Why:

- Vatican News reported almost 33.5 million pilgrims to Rome for the Jubilee
  Year.
- Rome is ideal for thematic routes, relics, basilicas and certificates.

What to do:

- Rome Seven Churches and Papal Basilicas are ideal premium-quality routes.
- Rome could become the first "deep premium" pack.

### 5. Brazil / Aparecida

Why:

- Android market share in Brazil was 80.01% in April 2026.
- Huge Catholic market.

Risk:

- Portuguese and local devotional tone need care.

### 6. Korea / WYD Seoul 2027

Why:

- World Youth Day Seoul is scheduled for August 3-8, 2027.
- Korea has a strong martyr story.

What to do:

- Do not compete with Pelegrin's community/chat angle.
- Own Korean martyr shrines, Seoul Catholic places, WYD pilgrimage passport and
  group certificates.

## What to build next

### High-confidence next feature: Pilgrim Mode for Manila Visita Iglesia

Reason:

- We already have Philippines content.
- It turns a route into a guided experience.
- It creates the right review/share moment.
- It is low-risk if built additively.
- It does not touch payments.

Minimal v1:

- route start panel,
- intention field saved locally,
- stop-by-stop route checklist,
- short prayer per stop,
- "mark visited" action reused from current logic,
- progress state,
- completion card,
- review/share prompt after completion.

### Second feature: activation analytics

Reason:

- Without event data, we are flying blind.
- We should know if users open routes, mark visits and share cards.

Rule:

- Track actions, not private text.
- Do not log journal content, prayer intentions, names, emails or exact sensitive
  notes.

### Third feature: Play Store custom listings and landing pages

Reason:

- Play's 2026 discovery tools reward precise user intent.
- Country/event landing pages make outreach easier.

First pages:

- Peregrin Philippines,
- Peregrin Holy Land,
- Peregrin Guadalupe.

## Product principles

- Add, do not destabilize.
- Make every pack feel local and devotional.
- Do not monetize faith; monetize memory, beauty, convenience and depth.
- Avoid generic Catholic content unless it is tied to a place or route.
- Prefer one polished route over 100 shallow places.
- Every serious route should end with something beautiful.
- Every campaign should have one measurable success metric.
- No social network until there is a clear operational reason.
- Use institutions and groups through outreach, not complex app features.

## 30-day experimental plan

### Week 1

- Add activation analytics plan.
- Confirm Play Console custom listing setup for Philippines.
- Prepare Play Store screenshots for Philippines.
- Draft landing page copy.

### Week 2

- Build Pilgrim Mode v1 for Manila Visita Iglesia.
- Include completion card and review trigger placeholder.

### Week 3

- Outreach to Filipino Catholic groups, creators, parish pages and pilgrimage
  communities.
- Offer premium codes for feedback, not reviews.
- Collect corrections and missing shrine suggestions.

### Week 4

- Read Console/Firebase data.
- Decide whether to polish Philippines, build Holy Land, or build Guadalupe.

## North star

Peregrin should become:

```text
The app a Catholic downloads before visiting a shrine.
The app they open while making the pilgrimage.
The app they keep because it remembers the journey beautifully.
```
