# Peregrin growth plan - 90 days

Last updated: 2026-05-23

## Executive thesis

Peregrin should not compete head-on with broad Catholic prayer apps.

Hallow, Amen, Ascension and Laudate own the daily-prayer, Bible, readings,
rosary and meditation space. Peregrin's strongest position is different:

```text
The Catholic pilgrim passport for real holy places.
```

That means Peregrin should become the app people use to discover, follow,
remember and share Catholic pilgrimages: shrines, routes, stamps, certificates
and personal pilgrimage memories.

The product should feel devotional, beautiful and practical, not touristy and
not like a generic Catholic content library.

## What the market says

### Big Catholic apps

- Hallow is the dominant Catholic prayer app, reporting over 23 million
  downloads and more than 1 billion prayers completed.
- Amen and Ascension compete around free prayer, Bible, daily readings,
  catechesis and audio content.
- Laudate remains a large free reference app with prayers, readings, rosary,
  catechism and many languages.

Conclusion:

Do not try to win by adding generic prayers, rosaries, readings or audio
meditation. Those are crowded lanes.

### Pilgrimage and passport apps

The more relevant competitive set is fragmented:

- Camino apps such as Wise Pilgrim, Buen Camino, Camino Ninja and JacoStamp
  focus on route logistics, stamps, maps and Camino-specific value.
- The Shell offers Franciscan pilgrimage content and a pilgrim passport/badge
  pattern.
- Diocesan pilgrimage passport apps exist, but are local and campaign-specific.
- Pelegrin is targeting Catholic youth and World Youth Day Seoul 2027 with a
  community-first approach.

Conclusion:

There is room for a global Catholic pilgrimage passport, especially if Peregrin
stays focused on place, memory and routes instead of trying to be a full social
network or a full travel planner.

## Product positioning

Primary positioning:

```text
Peregrin Passport
Your Catholic pilgrim passport for shrines, routes and holy memories.
```

Country/event listing pattern:

```text
Peregrin Philippines - Catholic Pilgrim Passport
Peregrin Holy Land - Catholic Pilgrim Passport
Peregrin Guadalupe - Catholic Pilgrim Passport
Peregrin Rome - Catholic Pilgrim Passport
Peregrin Korea 2027 - Catholic Pilgrim Passport
```

Core promise:

```text
Discover holy places. Mark your pilgrimages. Keep a beautiful Catholic passport.
```

What Peregrin should not sound like:

- A generic tourist guide.
- A cheap gamified religion app.
- A database of churches.
- A Hallow competitor.

## Strategic wedge

Use country and event packs as acquisition wedges.

Each pack should have:

- A custom Play Store listing.
- A simple landing page.
- 1 free hero route.
- 15-30 strong places, not hundreds.
- 2-3 routes people immediately understand.
- Shareable certificate or route-completion image.
- Outreach list of real communities and creators.

The app stays one app. The market sees many focused doors into it.

## Priority markets

### 1. Philippines

Status: launched as free content pack.

Why it matters:

- Very large Catholic population.
- Android-heavy market.
- Strong living pilgrimage traditions: Visita Iglesia, Santo Nino, Black
  Nazarene, Manaoag, Baclaran, Antipolo, Penafrancia.
- English is viable for the first listing, with Filipino/Tagalog copy reviewed
  by a native Catholic speaker later.

Goal:

Find out whether a country-specific Catholic passport can produce real
activation.

### 2. Holy Land

Why it matters:

- Universal Catholic resonance.
- Very strong identity: Bethlehem, Nazareth, Jerusalem, Holy Sepulchre,
  Gethsemane, Sea of Galilee, Capernaum.
- High emotional value even for virtual/aspirational pilgrims.

Risk:

Needs serious editorial care and sensitivity. Do not make it feel casual or
political.

Recommendation:

Make this the prestige pack, not a rushed add-on.

### 3. Mexico / Guadalupe

Why it matters:

- Huge Catholic market.
- Spanish gives us a natural execution advantage.
- Guadalupe is one of the strongest Catholic devotions in the world.

Recommendation:

After Philippines signal, build "Peregrin Guadalupe" as a Spanish-first pack.

### 4. Rome

Why it matters:

- Universal Catholic destination.
- Jubilee 2025 proved pilgrimage demand at enormous scale, with Vatican News
  reporting over 33 million pilgrims to Rome.
- Easier to create premium-quality routes: Seven Churches, Papal Basilicas,
  Vatican and martyrs.

Recommendation:

Rome can become the first polished premium pack once the acquisition loop works.

### 5. Korea / WYD Seoul 2027

Why it matters:

- Official WYD Seoul 2027 dates are August 3-8, 2027.
- There will be a global wave of Catholic youth preparing for Korea.
- Korea already exists in Peregrin as an Asian Catholic story around martyrs.

Risk:

Pelegrin is already targeting WYD with a community product. Peregrin should not
try to copy that. We can own "Catholic places and pilgrim passport", not chat.

Recommendation:

Prepare a Korea/WYD pack later, closer to the event, focused on shrines,
martyrs, Seoul pilgrimage stops and group certificates.

## Product gaps that matter

### 1. Shareable moments

This is the highest-impact product gap.

Users need beautiful things to share:

- Route completion cards.
- Country passport cards.
- "I completed Manila Visita Iglesia" image.
- "My Santo Nino Cebu pilgrimage" image.
- Certificate preview optimized for WhatsApp, Instagram Stories and Facebook.

Why:

Distribution through Catholic communities will be social. People need a reason
to post.

### 2. Activation analytics

Right now downloads alone are not enough.

We need privacy-respectful event data:

- App opened.
- Country selected.
- Sanctuary opened.
- Visit registered.
- Route opened.
- Route completed.
- Certificate generated.
- Share tapped.
- Premium modal opened.
- Premium purchased or restored.

Do not collect sensitive prayer text or journal content.

### 3. In-app review moment

Add Google Play in-app review only after a meaningful success moment:

- User registers 3 visits.
- User completes a route.
- User downloads/shares a certificate.

Never ask on first launch. Never incentivize reviews.

### 4. Landing pages

Each campaign needs a simple web page:

- Peregrin Philippines.
- Peregrin Holy Land.
- Peregrin Guadalupe.
- Peregrin Rome.

Each page should include:

- Title and promise.
- 3 route examples.
- Screenshots.
- Play Store button.
- Short FAQ.

### 5. Local pricing and offer structure

Keep discovery free. Monetize beauty, memory, convenience and depth:

- Premium certificates.
- Advanced memories.
- Offline packs.
- Special routes.
- Full country collections.
- Export/share formats.

Do not make the app feel like it is charging for faith.

## 90-day roadmap

### Days 1-7: Philippines launch instrumentation and store basics

Actions:

- Confirm Philippines is enabled in Play Console production countries.
- Create Philippines custom store listing.
- Use English-first listing.
- Use screenshots from Manila Visita Iglesia, Santo Nino Cebu, and Marian
  Shrines of Luzon.
- Set Philippines local Premium price test point.
- Create 30-50 promo codes for real users.
- Prepare one simple landing page.

Do not:

- Build a second app.
- Add another country before we can measure Philippines.
- Change payment architecture.

Success signal:

- Philippines listing gets real impressions.
- At least 10-20 real Filipino users open a sanctuary or route.
- At least 5 users register a visit or send direct feedback.

### Days 8-21: Outreach and feedback

Actions:

- Contact 30 Catholic creators/groups/pages.
- Offer free premium codes for feedback, not reviews.
- Message parishes/groups around:
  - Visita Iglesia.
  - Santo Nino.
  - Baclaran.
  - Manaoag.
  - Antipolo.
- Ask for corrections and missing local shrines.
- Record every feedback item in a simple feedback log.

Success signal:

- At least 5 meaningful replies.
- At least 2 people give content corrections.
- At least 1 creator or group agrees to try/share.

### Days 22-45: Build the viral memory loop

Actions:

- Add one shareable completion card.
- Start with "Manila Visita Iglesia completed".
- Make it beautiful, devotional and lightweight.
- Add review prompt only after a successful completion/share moment.
- Improve route screenshots and Play Store assets.

Success signal:

- Users share completion images.
- App gets its first organic review from a real user.
- D1 retention improves after users open a route.

### Days 46-60: Decide the second wedge

Decision:

If Philippines shows activation, choose the next pack:

- Holy Land if we want prestige and universal spiritual appeal.
- Guadalupe/Mexico if we want Spanish-language reach and faster execution.
- Rome if we want premium pilgrimage depth.

Recommendation:

Pick Holy Land for brand prestige only if we have time to do it reverently.
Pick Guadalupe if we need faster growth and Spanish advantage.

### Days 61-90: Expand with one serious pack

Actions:

- Build one new pack, not three.
- Create custom listing and landing page.
- Add one free hero route.
- Add shareable completion image.
- Outreach to the relevant community.

Success signal:

- The second pack has a better activation rate than the first.
- Peregrin has a repeatable launch playbook.

## North-star metrics

Track these weekly:

- Store listing visitors.
- Installs by country.
- App opens.
- First sanctuary opened.
- First visit registered.
- First route opened.
- Route completion.
- Certificate generated.
- Share tapped.
- Review count and average rating.
- Premium purchase/restores.

Activation target:

```text
Install -> first sanctuary opened: 35%+
Install -> first visit registered: 10%+
Route opened -> route completion intent: 15%+
```

Early success threshold:

```text
100 real installs from a target country matter more than 1,000 random installs.
```

## What to build next

Recommended next engineering task:

```text
Build shareable route completion cards, starting with Manila Visita Iglesia.
```

Why this first:

- It creates organic distribution.
- It turns a private action into a public memory.
- It fits Peregrin's identity.
- It does not require payment changes.

Second engineering task:

```text
Add privacy-respectful analytics events for activation.
```

Why second:

- We cannot manage growth by looking only at downloads.
- We need to know whether users understand the app.

Third engineering task:

```text
Create landing pages for country packs.
```

Why third:

- Play Store alone is not enough.
- Landing pages give SEO and campaign links.
